Explore the Enchanting Garda Lake

From historic whitewashed towns and ancient olive groves to pristine beaches and delectable cuisine, experience the authentic charm of Garda Lake from your base at Villa Magellano.

The South of Garda Lake

Yours Trulli is located in the Valle d'Itria, one of Puglia's most picturesque areas, characterized by rolling hills, ancient olive trees, and the iconic trulli houses. This central location makes it the perfect base to explore the region's many treasures.

Within a short drive, you can discover charming towns like Alberobello, a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its dense concentration of trulli, or Ostuni, the "White City" perched dramatically on a hill with panoramic views of the countryside.

The coast is just 20 minutes away, offering pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. Inland, you'll find historic sites, nature reserves, and vineyards producing Puglia's renowned wines.

Charming Towns to Explore

Puglia is home to some of Italy's most picturesque towns, each with its own unique character and charm

Alberobello

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Alberobello is famous for its unique trulli houses with conical roofs. Explore the enchanting quarters of Rione Monti and Aia Piccola.

7 km from Yours Trulli

Ostuni

Known as the "White City" for its dazzling whitewashed buildings that cascade down a hillside. Wander through the labyrinthine streets and enjoy panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea.

15 km from Yours Trulli

Locorotondo

One of Italy's most beautiful villages, Locorotondo is a circular town with immaculate whitewashed houses, decorated balconies, and flower-filled streets.

8 km from Yours Trulli

Polignano a Mare

Perched on dramatic limestone cliffs above the Adriatic Sea, this coastal gem features a charming old town and the famous Lama Monachile beach nestled in a rocky cove.

35 km from Yours Trulli

Martina Franca

A baroque gem with elegant piazzas, intricate facades, and a lively atmosphere. Known for its prestigious summer opera festival and excellent local salumi.

12 km from Yours Trulli

Lecce

Often called the "Florence of the South," Lecce is renowned for its ornate baroque architecture, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. Don't miss the stunning Basilica di Santa Croce.

85 km from Yours Trulli

Pristine Beaches

Puglia boasts some of Italy's most beautiful coastlines, with crystal-clear waters and diverse beach experiences

Puglia features over 800 kilometers of coastline along both the Adriatic and Ionian seas

Torre Canne

Just 18 km from Yours Trulli, this family-friendly beach offers shallow waters, soft sand, and excellent facilities. The thermal baths here are renowned for their therapeutic properties.

Porto Selvaggio

A protected natural reserve with a rugged coastline and crystal-clear waters, perfect for snorkeling. Surrounded by pine forests, it offers a more wild and natural beach experience.

Punta Prosciutto

Often compared to Caribbean beaches, Punta Prosciutto features fine white sand and turquoise waters. It's worth the drive to experience one of Italy's most beautiful beaches.

Baia dei Turchi

A pristine beach near Otranto with fine sand and shallow waters, surrounded by Mediterranean pine forests. Named after the landing of Turkish troops in the 15th century.

Culinary Delights

Puglia's cuisine is renowned for its simplicity, freshness, and rich flavors, showcasing the region's agricultural bounty

Orecchiette

The region's iconic ear-shaped pasta, traditionally served with cime di rapa (turnip tops) or a rich tomato sauce. In Bari's old town, you can watch local women making fresh orecchiette by hand.

Burrata

A specialty of Andria, burrata is a rich, creamy cheese made from mozzarella and cream. The outer shell is solid mozzarella, while the inside contains soft, stringy curd and fresh cream.

Olive Oil

Puglia produces about 40% of Italy's olive oil, with some trees dating back over 1,000 years. The region's olive oil is prized for its fruity flavor and peppery finish.

Focaccia Barese

A thick, soft bread topped with cherry tomatoes, olives, and oregano. Bari's version is uniquely made with potato in the dough, creating a moist and fluffy texture.

Fresh Seafood

With its extensive coastline, Puglia offers exceptional seafood. Don't miss specialties like tiella (baked rice, potatoes, and mussels) or freshly caught sea urchins in coastal towns.

Primitivo Wine

This robust red wine is Puglia's most famous grape variety. Related to California's Zinfandel, it produces full-bodied wines with rich flavors of dark fruits and spices.

Experiences & Activities

Discover the many ways to experience Puglia's rich culture, history, and natural beauty

Olive Oil Tasting

Visit an ancient masseria (fortified farmhouse) with centuries-old olive trees to learn about the production process and taste different varieties of the region's "green gold."

Cooking Classes

Learn to prepare traditional Pugliese dishes with local chefs. From handmade pasta to classic tiella, unlock the secrets of the region's beloved cuisine.

Yacht Charters

Explore Puglia's stunning coastline by sea. Private boat tours offer access to hidden coves, grottoes, and beaches that can't be reached by land.

Cycling Tours

The rolling countryside of Valle d'Itria is perfect for cycling. Guided tours take you through ancient olive groves, vineyards, and charming villages off the beaten path.

Artisan Workshops

Try your hand at traditional crafts with local artisans. Learn pottery in Grottaglie, paper-mache in Lecce, or the art of basket weaving from village masters.

Wine Tasting

Discover the wines of Puglia at local vineyards and wineries. From robust Primitivo to elegant Negroamaro, experience guided tastings with paired local specialties.

Our concierge can arrange any of these experiences for you, with private guides and transportation if desired.
